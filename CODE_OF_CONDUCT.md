# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

codexa is an open, welcoming project. Everyone who participates —
whether you're reporting a bug, submitting a PR, asking a question,
or just reading the code — deserves to be treated with respect.

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, background, experience
level, nationality, or any other personal characteristic.

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ We encourage

- Helping others — especially newcomers
- Giving constructive, specific feedback on code and ideas
- Disagreeing respectfully — critique the idea, not the person
- Sharing knowledge and explaining your reasoning
- Saying "I don't know" or "I was wrong" — that's strength, not weakness

### ❌ We do not tolerate

- Insults, personal attacks, or dismissive language
- Harassment of any kind — public or private
- Posting others' private information without permission
- Spam, off-topic noise, or bad-faith arguments
- Sustained disruption of discussions or reviews

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, and Discussions
- Code review comments
- Any other space where you represent the codexa project

---

## Enforcement

If you see a violation or experience one yourself:

1. **Email:** your@email.com  
   Subject: `[codexa] Code of Conduct`
2. Reports are handled confidentially
3. We will respond within **72 hours**

### What happens after a report

| Severity | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Minor / first offence | Private warning with explanation |
| Repeated or serious | Temporary ban from the project |
| Severe (threats, harassment) | Permanent ban, no warning needed |

The project maintainer has the final say on all enforcement decisions.
We're not trying to punish people — we're trying to keep the space
productive and safe for everyone.

---

## For maintainers

When reviewing reports:

- Don't ignore them — even a quick "received, looking into it" matters
- Be consistent — same rules apply to everyone including long-time contributors
- Document your decision privately even if you don't share it publicly
